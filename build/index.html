Patient Health Questionnaire (PHQ-9)

The PHQ-9 is a widely used screening tool for assessing the severity of depression. It consists of 9 questions that evaluate the frequency of symptoms associated with depression, such as loss of interest, feelings of guilt or worthlessness, and suicidal thoughts. Each question is scored on a scale from 0 (not at all) to 3 (nearly every day), and the total score ranges from 0 to 27. A score of 5 or higher indicates the presence of depression, with higher scores indicating more severe symptoms. The PHQ-9 is commonly used in primary care settings and can help healthcare providers determine the appropriate course of treatment for patients experiencing depression.
